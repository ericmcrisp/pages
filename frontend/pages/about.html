Career

I attended Pennsylvania State University and earned a B.S. in Aerospace Engineering, with the majority of my expeirenced focused in research related to combustion and combustion-engine based testing. After Penn State, I began a research position with the Air Force Research Lab in Dayton, OH where I pursued research in various hypersonic combustion research projects. This research was intended to be tied to a Masters thesis through the University of Cincinnati, however, this did not work out according to plan so I continued my M.S. at Penn State where I finished coursework and completed a thesis on a separate topic - hypergolic ignition of ionic liquids for deep-space applications. Upon graduating in 2021, I moved to Austin, Texas to work for the (at the time) start up Firefly Aerospace as an Aerospace Engineer (Propulsion Developement Engineer) focused on Combustion Devices (i.e., the engines). After great success on the launch vechile engines as well as a full design and production of the lunar engine, I left Firefly to become an Engine System Analyst developing the real-time modeling capabilities at Blue Origin. This was my role for almost three years until I left to pivot career focueses. That is when I applied to various AI research and AI/ML engineer positions. I decided to pursue a M.S. in Data Science at the University of Pennsylvania to cover much of the computer science fundamentals I had missed in my previous education and work exposure.
